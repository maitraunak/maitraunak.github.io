Exploring Economics,
Society, and Human Behaviour.

I studied Economics (Honours) at Presidency University, Kolkata, and am currently pursuing my MA in Applied Economics at the Centre for Development Studies, JNU, Trivandrum.

I started out as a PCMB student, but a growing curiosity about how societies function gradually drew me towards economics. Since then, I have been fascinated by understanding how policy, technology, and institutions influence economic and social outcomes. My current interests include exploring the impact of digitalisation on firm performance and the role of education in intimate partner violence. I am also interested in mental health and well-being, behavioural norms, crime against sexually marginalised communities, and geospatial econometrics.

I come from a background marked by poverty and limited resources, which has fundamentally shaped how I think about inequality, opportunity, and access. I know what it feels like to pursue education without a safety net. To constantly navigate uncertainty while trying to move forward.Those experiences have deeply influenced the questions I ask as an explorer and the topics I choose to study.

Personal Journal

From an unknown village
to Presi to JNU — and still going.

These are notes I write to myself. About the distance between where I come from and where I am now. About the things nobody tells you when you're the first in your family to go this far. About grief, money, ambition, loneliness — and occasionally, something that feels like clarity.

I grew up in Baharampur, Murshidabad — a small town in West Bengal where getting into Presidency University already felt like an unlikely thing. Getting to JNU after that felt even more so. The path here was not straight, and it was not easy. There were years of real financial difficulty — the kind where you count what things cost before you decide whether to show up. There was grief I carried quietly, alongside coursework and ambitions that had no obvious precedent in my immediate world.

I am writing this journal because I needed something like it when I was starting out, and it didn't exist. Not about economics — about what it actually feels like to come from very little and try to build something, one semester at a time. If you are from a similar place, moving through similar terrain, I hope something here is useful to you. You are not alone in this.
